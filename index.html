Dungeon Of Rhythm

A rhythm based dungeon crawler game designed by Lewis Cubed Studios:
Lewis Heath Mozamil Sadat Lewis Powell Chris Jobling Lewis Baillie

Description

Dungeon of Rhythm is a rhythm-based dungeon crawler game. You are trapped inside a dungeon with no idea which direction to go. Fight through the onslaught of enemies to find the end-room. Within the end-room you will see the deadly purple dragon, this is the last and hardest challenge you have to face before leaving the dungeon. The key to success is the rhythm. Before starting, the user picks a song from the menu they wish to play the game too. Listen closely to the beat of the song because moving on the beat gives you extra score. Attacking on the beat deals more damage to your opponents. The closer to the beat the higher the score and damage dealt. Look out for different weapons scattered around the map as you can use them in your journey to escape.

Key Game Mechanics

Beat map system

By breaking down the song, the rhythm and therefore every beat can be detected and used in the game for movement, attacking and enemy movement.

Procedurally generated map

Each time the game is played each room in the dungeon is procedurally generated. Consequently, a different map is produced every time the user plays.

Combat system

Different weapons can be equipped that do different damage and different spread on attack. The axe can hit multple enemies at a wider range than the sword and dagger.

Enemy AI

The enemies are programmed carefully in a way where they only move to the beats. Some move every beat, some move every 2 beats. They move towards the player and when within range attack.

Final boss

The dangerous purple dragon lurks in the last room of the dungeon, it is the only thing left standing in your way to escape!

Weapon pickups

Look around the map for glowing weapons, they can really help in the treacherous journey through the dungeon.                           

Interactive User Interface

Dont worry you are not alone! The interactive user interface will guide you along the way making everything clear so you dont get too confused.
